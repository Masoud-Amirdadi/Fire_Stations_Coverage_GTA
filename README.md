This interactive GIS-based visualization tool presents an integrated view of fire-service coverage for the cities of Toronto and Mississauga within the Greater Toronto Area (GTA), Ontario, Canada. Developed as part of an applied optimization and spatial-analytics framework, the tool brings together geospatial modeling, service-area estimation, and data-driven decision-support methods in a lightweight, browser-based platform.

The system incorporates buffer-based isochrones and Voronoi-based partitions to model and compare fire-station influence areas. Two scenarios are included:

Distance-Only Service Areas, representing traditional proximity-based coverage.

Distance + Incident-Intensity Service Areas, where historical emergency-incident density reshapes the polygons to better capture operational demand.

Comparing these scenarios allows users to explore how geography, density, and station workload affect effective coverage across the two municipalities.
